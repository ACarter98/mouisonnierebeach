About Mouisonniere Beach

Mouisonniere Beach represents Guernsey's wilder side. This small, shingle-and-sand cove sits beneath steep cliffs, accessed only by a winding coastal path. The effort to reach it is rewarded with a sense of discovery that busier beaches can't match.

The beach faces south-east, catching morning sunshine that illuminates the surrounding cliffs in golden light. It's a favorite spot for photographers and those seeking solitude.

Wildlife Haven

The cliffs above host nesting seabirds in spring - fulmars, herring gulls, and the occasional peregrine falcon. Binoculars recommended. The beach itself is a geologist's dream, with folded rock layers visible in the cliff faces.

Tide Dependent

Mouisonniere transforms dramatically with the tides. At low water, a sandy beach is revealed with fascinating rock pools. At high tide, only shingle remains - still beautiful, but very different in character.

Visitor Tips

  • Check tide times - beach changes completely
  • Wear sturdy shoes for the path
  • Bring everything - nearest shop is 1 mile away
  • Seals sometimes visit - keep distance
  • No mobile signal - plan accordingly
